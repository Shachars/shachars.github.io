Dimension Current state — what's missing IEEE 802.11 AI Offload — what it adds
AI capability advertisement No network-layer mechanism to announce AI compute capabilities. Devices rely on mDNS or proprietary app-layer discovery. AP beacon and probe frames carry AI types, modalities (text, image, voice, video), and performance parameters natively.
Session & authorization No AI session concept in 802.11. Model loading, resource allocation, and security must be handled by each application separately. Structured setup request/response with service hash, session ID, security keys, and model URL — integrated into WPA3 framework.
Inference data transport AI runtimes (ONNX, TFLite) are application-layer only. No standardized format for exchanging input/output vectors over Wi-Fi. Standardized transport for inference vectors in protobuf and ONNX. Vendor-neutral across all edge compute platforms.
Model certification No industry-wide requirements for model quality, quantization, or size. Edge models are entirely self-certified by publishers. Formal certification framework: quantization standards, fixed-point granularity, max model size — enforced at download time.
Agent-to-agent comms Agentic AI (A2A, MCP) has no wireless network-layer support. Agents rely on ad-hoc application routing with no standardized discovery. 802.11 discovery extended to support A2A and MCP protocols, enabling distributed agentic AI across Wi-Fi infrastructure.
