About me

  • I am an Algorithm Researcher at Huawei's Tel Aviv Research Center, specializing in wireless communications algorithms for WiFi. My work focuses on a range of Physical Layer (PHY) and Medium Access Control (MAC) topics, including iterative MIMO detection algorithms and Deep Reinforcement Learning for WiFi congestion control.

  • Beyond my role at Huawei, I am a lecturer at Tel Aviv University's Electrical Engineering School, where I teach the graduate course Digital Transmission of Signals.

  • With over a decade of experience in developing cutting-edge algorithms for communications and sensing, I have held research and engineering positions at leading companies, including Intel, Vayyar Imaging, Corning Optical Communications, General Motors Research, and Apple.

  • I hold a PhD in Electrical Engineering from Tel Aviv University, where I focused on Information Theoretic Active Learning under the supervision of Prof. Meir Feder.
